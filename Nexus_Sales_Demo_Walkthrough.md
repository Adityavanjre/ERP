STEP 2: THE CLIENT RELATIONSHIPS (Customers)
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Customers
Action: Click on a Customer name inside the table to view their profile.
What to Say: To scale operations, we need client control. Pull up any client profile, and view their overall debt caps, historical orders, and active credit lines on one single screen.
Why it Matters: Combats bad debt triggers by giving sales reps immediate visual alerts if a client tries to over-book Orders beyond authorized credit balances.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 3: SETTING THE DEAL (Sales Orders)
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Orders
Action: Click on a Staged or Draft sales order to view current quotes.
What to Say: For larger accounts, you stage orders. This serves as a safety filter holding goods in reserved states without releasing warehouse stock until managers authorize execution.
Why it Matters: Safegaurds that priority materials aren't speed-shipped to alternative buyers by accident, reserving optimal cash-flow.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 4: SPEED INVOICING (Quick Sale)
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Quick Sale
Action: Click the Quick Sale button, type any product code, and look at the invoice builder area.
What to Say: For walk-in counter releases, making an invoice takes 3 seconds. Quick Sale auto-fetches rates so staff can’t sell below target gross margins.
Why it Matters: Prevents human error during bulk invoices and allows high-threshold retail counter traffic without slowing down local operations.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 5: HANDLING RETURNS (Credit Notes)
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Credit Notes
Action: Click Create Credit Note to view the dispute filing form.
What to Say: Returns happen. Standardize your refunds by generating a Credit Note that auto-settles outstanding client invoices and Restores correct shelf stock limits.
Why it Matters: Removes manual calculations from accounting closures. Keeps refund cycles audit-proof to prevent floating credit vulnerabilities.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 6: SMART CATALOGING (Item / Product)
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Item/Product
Action: Click on any item listing view to see inventory dimensions and safety thresholds.
What to Say: Manage items thoroughly. Nexus tracks materials with precise attributes and minimum safety limits, firing automatically triggers that prevent running dry.
Why it Matters: Alleviates expensive stockouts. Sets optimal carrying costs so capital isn't locked on shelves doing nothing.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 7: PROCUREMENT TRIGGERS (Purchases)
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Purchases
Action: View an active Purchase Order listing on the screen.
What to Say: When stocks diminish, Nexus channels into Purchasing, preparing procurement orders for raw goods so your supply chain remains continuously moving.
Why it Matters: Standardizes supplier communication. Allows easy inspection of what is Ordered vs Paid before goods arrive.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 8: DISPUTES GUARD (Debit Notes)
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Debit Notes
Action: Click Create Debit Note to inspect the withholding form.
What to Say: If raw material arrives damaged or short, generate a Debit Note. It instructs your general bookkeeper to withhold that specific sum from the Vendor payment.
Why it Matters: Direct financial dispute guarding. Restricts double-paying for failed deliveries.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 9: MULTI-BIN LOGISTICS (Stock Bins)
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Stock 
Action: View the stock list grid showing Bins or Warehouse locations.
What to Say: Nexus supports exact multi-warehouse architectures. You can inspect exact locations what is on shelf, in transit, or currently placed in WIP bins.
Why it Matters: Solves ghost inventory issues where materials belong in the plant but no one knows what specific rack they sit on.

STEP 10: IMMUTABLE LOGS (Stock Movements)
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Stock Movements

What to Say: Every item moved from A to B generates an immutable log. You get highly granular traceability keyed to dates, times, and employees.
