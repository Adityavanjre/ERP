NEXUS ERP SCENARIO-DRIVEN SALES DEMO WALKTHROUGH
SCENARIO: FROM SALES DEMAND TO COMPLETED MANUFACTURING & ACCOUNTING CLOSURES

==================================================================
PHASE 1: DEMAND INTAKE & ORDER MANAGEMENT (The Sales Workflow)
Focus: Demonstrating how Nexus handles custom pricing and pipelines.
==================================================================

SCENE 1: THE COMMAND CENTER
Direction/Path: Go to Main Dashboard
Action: Hover over the Active Work Orders card and overall cash widgets on the screen.
Why it Matters: Standard factories lag 15 days in the past trying to compile manual totals. Nexus streams accurate updates immediately so you manage cash positions with zero blindspots.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 2: THE CLIENT PROFILING
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Customers
Action: Click on any Customer Row in the table to view their account and debt limits.
Why it Matters: Combats bad debt triggers by giving sales reps immediate visual alerts if a client tries to book an Order beyond authorized credit balances.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 3: THE DEMAND CAPTURE
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Orders
Action: Click on a Staged or Draft sales order to view current quote items.
Why it Matters: Safegaurds that priority materials arenot speed-shipped to alternative buyers by accident, reserving optimal cash-flow.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 4: AUXILIARY SPEED BILLING
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Quick Sale
Action: Click the Quick Sale button, type any product code, and look at the invoice builder.
Why it Matters: Prevents human error during bulk invoices and allows high-threshold retail counter traffic without slowing down local operations.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 5: HANDLING DISPUTES
Direction/Path: Go to Sales -> Credit Notes
Action: Click Create Credit Note to view the dispute filing form.
Why it Matters: Keeps refund cycles audit-proof to prevent floating credit vulnerabilities without manual arithmetic.

==================================================================
PHASE 2: SUPPLY CHAIN VERIFICATION (The Inventory Workflow)
Focus: Ensuring raw materials are prepped before production lines halt.
==================================================================

SCENE 6: SMART CATALOGS
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Item/Product
Action: Click on any Item listing to see dimensions and minimum threshold alerts.
Why it Matters: Alleviates expensive stockouts. Sets optimal carrying costs so capital isnnot locked on shelves doing nothing.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 7: MULTI-BIN LOGISTICS
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Stock 
Action: View the stock list grid showing Bins or Warehouse locations.
Why it Matters: Solves ghost inventory issues where materials belong in the plant but no one knows what specific rack they sit on.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 8: PROCUREMENT TRIGGERS
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Purchases
Action: View an active Purchase Order listing on the screen.
Why it Matters: Allows easy inspection of what is Ordered vs Paid before goods arrive to avoid double-allocation.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 9: DISPUTE DISCHARGE
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Debit Notes
Action: Click Create Debit Note to inspect the withholding form.
Why it Matters: Direct financial dispute guarding. Restricts paying for failed deliveries.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 10: IMMUTABLE LOGS
Direction/Path: Go to Inventory -> Stock Movements
Action: View the log list showing IN and OUT adjustments.
Why it Matters: Zero-latency defense against internal theft or un-audited shrinkage.

==================================================================
PHASE 3: AUTOMATED FABRICATION (The Manufacturing Workflow)
Focus: Converting raw items into high-margin Finished Goods.
==================================================================

SCENE 11: PRODUCTION COCKPIT
Direction/Path: Go to Manufacturing -> Overview
Action: Show overall production dashboards or yield metrics on the screen.
Why it Matters: Perfect for rapid analyst setups representing line efficiency without loading complicated spreadsheets.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 12: ASSET TARGETING
Direction/Path: Go to Manufacturing -> Machines
Action: Look at the machine listing table. Note the status is Idle.
Why it Matters: Predicts breakdown windows through asset hour tracking triggers while allocating accurate hourly running weights.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 13: RECIPES FOR COST
Direction/Path: Go to Manufacturing -> BOM
Action: Open the BOM list and click on row 1 for example Silk Shirt asset.
Why it Matters: Solves underpricing blindspots. Tracks exact margin absorption factors safely before committing expensive orders.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 14: SAFETY CONSTRAINTS
Direction/Path: Go to Manufacturing -> Orders -> Create New
Action: Select a BOM template, set quantity high like 100, and show the Warning alert missing materials box.
Why it Matters: Starting a line only to halt midpoint wastes hours and operator wages. Nexus prevents bottlenecks automatically.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 15: THE EXECUTABLE RUN
Direction/Path: Go to Manufacturing -> Orders
Action: Click on any existing Order and click Mark Complete button.
Why it Matters: Eliminates hours. In one tap, Inventory and Ledger balances update safely and concurrently, moving values to assets.

==================================================================
PHASE 4: CORPORATE SEALING (The Accounting Workflow)
Focus: Live ledger accuracy, Compliance audits, and P&L readouts.
==================================================================

SCENE 16: HEAVY CAPITAL Depreciations
Direction/Path: Go to Accounting -> Fixed Assets
Action: View the assets list covering machines and properties.
Why it Matters: Secures optimal taxation returns based on truthful depreciation declarations for heavy equipment.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 17: UNIFIED DOUBLE-ENTRY
Direction/Path: Go to Accounting -> Accounts
Action: Scroll down the Chart of Accounts list on the screen.
Why it Matters: Protects cash statements immediately without reconciling offline journals at month-end.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 18: COMPLIANCE SCANS
Direction/Path: Go to Accounting -> Auditor
Action: Point to the Auditor menu options or verification scans on the screen.
Why it Matters: Extreme internal defense keeping books bulletproof and correcting transaction discrepancies before standard auditing.

--------------------------------------------------

SCENE 19: THE GRAND CLOSING
Direction/Path: Go to Accounting -> Reports
Action: Click and Display active Profit and Loss sheet or Balance Sheet.
Why it Matters: Absolute high C-suite visibility on where budget leaks or cash flow strengths sit.
